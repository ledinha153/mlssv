Music

Music is where my emotions breathe — it understands me when words don’t.

Music has always been more than just sound to me. It’s how I process my feelings, sit with my thoughts, and feel understood without having to explain anything. Some days I don’t feel like talking at all, and music is the only thing that stays—quietly filling the space without asking for anything back.

I’m drawn to songs that feel soft, emotional, and honest—the kind that don’t scream but stay with you. Artists like Taylor Swift, Cigarettes After Sex, Bruno Mars, and Sabrina Carpenter speak to different parts of me: vulnerability, longing, reflection, and quiet strength.

Sometimes I listen for comfort, sometimes for healing, and sometimes just so I don’t feel alone. Certain songs hold memories, moods, and whole phases of my life.

And every now and then, I go back to older songs—music from another era. If a song feels real, no matter when it was made, it always finds its way into my world.
